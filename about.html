About Me

I am a brittle asthmatic sufferer, have been since I was two years old. But since I was 18, I have taken pride in helping others in my situation so people don't go through this alone.

I was one of the youngest patients to be put on the treatment Xolair. The dose is worked out for each patient on their IgE levelsl I have four injections, every two weeks.

I love helping others, whether it is to do with understanding a situation, making other people feel at ease or helping them in any way I can. That is what prompted the blog and community page.

Although I am allergic, I pride myself with the care I give to my animals. Possibly not the most healthiest of choices, but they are my mental strength.

I think it is so important to have an outlet when you are dealing with a chronic illness. If that is art, animals, running or dancing. Something to take your mind off of it. Your illness doesn't define you!

I use to be terrified of needles! I wouldn't let anyone near me with one without screaming. But being on Xolair has really helped with my anxiety over needles and injections.

Ralph

Ralph is the reason I have kept going through the weakest times. Ralph is a Welsh Section D who has been in my life for 10 years.

Poppy

Poppy is my second reason! She was gifted to me as a rescue when she was a baby. She also has lots of health problems, so we help each other out.

Patch

Patch helped me learn to walk again when I spent my first long stint in hospital and loss all leg muscle.
